2 · Collect Examples

Pick a class, then hold the capture button while moving the object around — 30–50 varied shots per class works well. You can also upload image files per class above.

Tip: change angle, distance, background and lighting while holding — variety beats quantity.
Examples are converted to compact feature vectors on your GPU as you capture, so training later takes only seconds. Raw images stay in this page's memory only — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

4 · Test Live & Export

Point the camera at your objects — predictions update in real time

Detect mode: a general-purpose detector (COCO-SSD, running in your browser) finds objects and draws boxes; your freshly trained classifier names each box with YOUR classes. Works best with everyday objects the detector already recognizes (bottles, phones, boxes, tools, people...).
Export downloads your trained model (TensorFlow.js format: model.json + weights) together with classes.txt. To run it in your own website, load it with tf.loadLayersModel('model.json') together with MobileNet features — the same free libraries this page uses.
